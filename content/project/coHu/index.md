This project is part of the research training group [coHu](https://cohu.fau.eu/) at Friedrich-Alexander University Erlangen-Nürnberg. My subproject uses inverse optimal control to develop computational models of human motion in physically collaborative tasks involving contact interaction with robotic systems. It aims to capture how humans move, coordinate, and express intent during actions such as grasping, handover, or tool use. It combines biomechanical principles with data-driven and optimization-based modeling to infer the objectives underlying human movement. These models enable prediction and simulation of human behavior in collaborative environments for assistive robotics design.
