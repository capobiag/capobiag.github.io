Accurately modeling, simulating, and controlling frictional contact is vital for walking robots, robot manipulators, driver-assistance systems, exoskeletons, and understanding human movement. It is the goal to leverage on the event-capturing approach from nonsmooth mechanics to develop numerical methods for optimal control and MPC. This holistic contact description circumvents the need for assumptions about contact sequences or impact timing, enhancing robustness and versatility over traditional smooth regularization or hybrid methods.
